Important Books To Have.

This is Rubank.

.

This is the first book she will use. Rubank method books are kind of a classic for flute players — almost everyone uses them at some point. The Rubank Elementary Method is usually the first one people start with because it explains things in a simple, step‑by‑step way. It covers your first notes, basic rhythms, and easy exercises that help you build tone and confidence without feeling overwhelming. Once you get the hang of the basics, the Rubank Intermediate Method takes things a little further with more scales, faster rhythms, and short duets that make practicing more interesting. Even though these books have been around forever, they’re still super helpful because they’re organized, clear, and actually make you better if you stick with them.

This is my favorite first fun book 101 Disney Songs

Having fun books is actually really important when you’re learning an instrument. Practice can get repetitive sometimes, so having music you actually enjoy—like Disney songs, pop tunes, or familiar melodies—keeps you motivated. Fun books give you a break from exercises and scales while still helping you improve things like rhythm, tone, and sight‑reading. They make practice feel less like a chore and more like something you want to do. When you mix fun music with your regular method books, you learn faster, stay more confident, and actually look forward to picking up your flute.

This is a top‑bound spiral notebook

A top‑bound spiral notebook is one of those simple things that ends up being super useful, especially for music lessons. Because the spiral is at the top instead of the side, the pages flip upward and stay out of the way while you write. It’s great for taking quick notes during practice, writing down reminders, or keeping track of what you’re working on each week. A lot of students like this style because it feels neat, organized, and easy to hold with one hand. It’s basically the perfect little notebook for lessons, practice logs, or anything you want to keep track of without the pages getting bent or messy.
